What the app does

BavarianTranslator is a two-way, real-time conversation translator. The screen splits in half — Deutsch · Oma on top and English below. One person speaks, and the conversation appears in both languages at once. It’s specifically tuned to understand Bavarian dialect (Boarisch), and it can speak the German translation slowly so an elderly listener can follow.

Getting started

Translation is powered by a cloud AI that you choose. The app uses your own free API key, so your usage stays private and free. It takes about a minute:

  1. Get a free API key (no credit card) from one of:
    • Google Gemini — aistudio.google.com/apikey (best at dialect)
    • Groq — console.groq.com/keys (largest free allowance)
    • Mistral — console.mistral.ai/api-keys
  2. Open the app, tap ⚙︎ Settings → Translation engine, pick an engine, and paste the key.
  3. Go back, choose a mode, and start talking.
💡 You can add keys for more than one engine. If your chosen engine hits its daily limit, the app automatically switches to the next best one so your conversation never stops.

Helpful settings

  • Slow German for Oma — Settings → German speech for Oma. Choose Sehr langsam, Langsam, or Mittel so the spoken German is easy to follow.
  • Face-to-face — flips the German half upside-down so the person across the table reads it the right way up.
  • Sensitivity (Auto & Live) — if it cuts you off or misses a quiet talker, set it more Sensitive; if it triggers on background noise, set it Strict.
  • Replay — tap any line (🔊) to hear it spoken again.

Frequently asked questions

Is the app free? Do I have to pay?
The app itself is free. Translation uses a cloud AI through a free API key you create — no credit card required. The free tiers have generous daily limits that are plenty for everyday conversations.
Why do I need to add my own API key?
It keeps the app free and your conversations private: requests go directly from your phone to the AI provider using your key. Nothing is routed through us.
Which engine is best for Bavarian dialect?
In our testing, Google Gemini handled dialect best, while Groq proved very robust on real Bavarian and has the largest free allowance, and Mistral is strong on clear German. Each engine shows a quality score in Settings so you can compare, and the app auto-switches if one is unavailable.
Is my conversation private?
Your audio is sent only to the translation provider you choose, using your own key, purely to produce the translation. We don’t collect, store, or see your audio or translations. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.
It said “didn’t catch that.” What do I do?
Speak a little closer and clearly. In Auto/Live, try lowering the Sensitivity to “Sensitive” in Settings. Background noise and long silences can also trip it up.
It switched engines on its own — is that normal?
Yes. When an engine reaches its daily or per-minute limit, the app briefly shows “⚡ switched to …” and continues on the next best engine so your conversation isn’t interrupted. The limited engine re-enables itself automatically once its limit resets.
Does it work offline?
Translation needs an internet connection (it’s a cloud AI call). The spoken German voice itself works offline.

Troubleshooting

  • No translation appears — check your internet connection and confirm you added an API key in Settings → Translation engine.
  • “API key rejected” — re-copy the key (no extra spaces) and paste it again.
  • “Every engine hit its rate limit” — wait a little (limits reset within the hour, some overnight), or add a key for another engine so it can switch.
